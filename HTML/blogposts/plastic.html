Plastic

Not proofread, I can't guarnatee that it's comprehensible. Don't read if you're sensitive around topics of abuse.

There is always a question of genuineness hovering around every utterance ever made, not just by humans, but everything under our sun. Every measurement has to be verified, every stated thing fact checked, everything with its is dotted and ts crossed. All in service of perhaps the grandest cult in humanity. Objectivity.

I hate the word objective, I truly loathe it, it's one of the few words that can, on its own, truly agitate me. It's not like factoid, commonly misused, or a slur, which causes distress and offense. Objective is a word that gnashes against my entire world view. It's a word that feels like it violates my entire existence, our entire existence. Our as in everything, every single thing, human, snake, mountain, star, molecule, atom and quark. It feels violating to merely hear it. As if it suggests that there can even be such a thing as truth.

One of the most horrifying things to me is what I call the problem of perfect information. One can never truly know everything. One can never truly know what is and isn't. We talk of could haves and should haves but you can never know if doing x or doing y would have caused z. What if that could have, if followed through would have doomed the entire human race. It's unknowable, unfalsifiable, it's an utter nightmare upon my existence. Every time I think of doing something I war game out the possibilities, eventually reaching the same conclusion for the nth time that the only way to figure out what happens is to do it. And I can never know what happened if I didn't do it. Or I don't do it, and can never know what would happen if I did do it.

We give each other advice incessantly not knowing if that advice will bear fruit, we fully accept the possibility that our advice may end up being a disaster, and we treasure in the unfalsifiability, we deflect and forgive. Is it not a form of assault to give terrible advice? Or is it the fault of the person for acting out the advice? How can you know that giving the advice was what caused an action? What if the airwaves from you speaking out the advice caused a tornado to form in a different country killing 7 people.

One may think that the only solution to this problem is death. To leave this horrific mortal world of chaos and entropy. But beyond the grave you reverberate, and your influence persists. And people will ask if the tears of sorrow are cried truly for your untimely passing or if it was merely a coincidence. If they'd be happy if you were here. Or perhaps, if it wasn't for your death, they'd be even sadder. And voluntary death is accepting that the future cannot be better. It is acting with finality on something you have no right or possibility to judge. Death is the opposite of release and emancipation. Death is restraining yourself to 1 single fate, it's giving up entropy. Our flame brought by our Prometheus, the big bang.

Genuineness also expresses itself in my doubts of doing things properly. Am I trans right, am I a girl right, am I a Buddhist right, am I a human right. People can put a question mark on any facet of me and send me into a spiral. It feels unfair that only I get to feel this way. I arrogantly assume. A disgusting thought that I say to myself is false (yet at the same time, unprovable!) and instead of dismissing it like a normal person. I blame that it is the cognitive patterns described by a person described as a psychiarist as OCD causing me an inability to dismiss them. Again, unprovable.

The world feels like plastic, slippery and artificial. I cannot grasp it, both in the sense of comprehension and as something to hold and steady myself on. It scares and confuses me. The anger around me. If I walk down the street some person can come and decide to end my life. This will probably never happen, but it can happen, and that it can happen is enough for my mind to say that it will happen. And if it will happen, that means I need to do what I think can be done, but ultimately it is impossible to know if it can to prevent it. To prevent my imagined death by the hands of an imagined person, whom I assume is a man, and then I start thinking I am a terrible person for pushing gender stereotypes on an imaginary person. But then I deflect and say to myself it's not my fault, I've had many unpleasant experiences with men, besides this is a well repeated fact that most violence is committed by men. And then I say to myself that's merely deflection, and besides it's impossible to know remember? What if we don't know, what if all the violent men were misidentified as men, what if they were trans women in the closet, what if, what if this is all a Boltzmann brain and nothing fucking matters yeah, what if we just all instantly vapourise in the next instant wouldn't that be fucking grand?!

My father used to lock me in my room when I didn't behave. One of the reasons for locking me in was when I made too much noise denying that I had stolen some candy. It was, to my knowledge, not me, I believe that it was my brother who had scapegoated me. So I said to my dad, it wasn't me, please. I start crying and they laugh at me, my family, they say if I wasn't guilty I wouldn't be crying. I cry more, I say no, it wasn't me I promise, it's not fair. My dad tells me to shut up, and he pulls me by my ear. Before I know it I'm laying in my bed, in my overly warm jumper. I hear the birds outside and the shadow from the plum tree swaying in the wind come through the window. My eyes are still wet and irritated from the crying, but I lay there, looking up. I am not deep in thought, I am probably thinking about how I want to play on my Game Boy, or about how mean my brothers are. My dad doesn't actually lock me, the lock in my room was jammed for as long as I can remember. It was just slamming the door on me and a vague threat that I'm not allowed to leave. And I don't.

A few months ago I was visiting my parents. It was only my mother and one of my brothers. I am tired and I lay in the bed in my old bedroom. It's a new bed and it's in a different part of the room. But the window and the plum tree is the same. Well, not the same, but it's still a plum tree, from the same sapling, bearing similar tasting plums. I think about getting locked in the room a lot of the time, but not every time I enter my old childhood room, but I don't remember if I did this time. I lay in bed, I'm sad, I don't know why, I'm sad and tired. My mum calls out for dinner. I don't get up. She enters the room and holds around me. and the moment my mother touches me I start sobbing uncontrollably. She stands there, incapable of saying anything because we never emotionally communicated when I was younger, and we don't do it now either. So she stands there, awkwardly, caressing my cheek from behind. Saying she'll bring me food, and leaves. She comes back and puts a plate of chicken and rice and some other stuff on the desk, I get up and eat it. The sauce she has the chicken in I've had a lot and usually it tastes good, but this time it tasted flavourless. The chicken felt like I was chewing paper. The rice felt like eating dust. But I eat, I eat, I drink from the can of sparkling water she brought me and it tasted like liquid static and I think about……my ex.

She pops in my head, like she often does. A girl I dated for 3 years. As I am writing this right now it is almost precisely a year since we broke up. Whenever I think about her my mood absolutely sinks like a chunk of lead in a freshwater lake. But that day, as if fate was toying with me. She adds me on discord.

I was thinking about you just now.

I was too Daniella. If you're ever in Berlin, would you like to get a cup of coffee with me? I think both of us would benefit from face to face talk.

That is the last thing I would want.

That's okay, I understand.

Why? Why did you add me? Surely there is something you wish to bring up.

You were on my mind, and I wanted to reach out to you. Earlier today something happened to me and you were the first person I wanted to tell it to. I'm sorry I impulsively added you.

What happened.

I had a psychotic break unfortunately.

Your twitter is already psychotic enough.

And your bluesky is completely in the dark.

I do not use it.

You read my twitter still?

Sometimes. When I'm in a bad mood. You'll find I'm a very different person from a year ago.

I'll assure you, I am not the same girl stuck in her bedroom all day either..

You honestly seem worse.

I am changing. But it's scary sometimes.

What's so changed.

I hope you're doing better though.

I'm not.

Ever since I began my masters I've been basically non-stop reading I have to guess. It's hard to be better in 2026.

I'm dying

To this day I do not know why I told her that I was dying. I did it on a whim, I had already decided to block her and never talk to her again. She seemed to believe me. And I want her to. I want her to forget about me. I don't want to list every misdeed she ever did but let it not be unsaid that she did unspeakable things to me. "Abusive" and "rapist" feel like light terms.

Plastic.

I felt like her plastic toy. Bleached and discoloured, covered in scratches.

But I also disgust myself at my own self-victimisation. I spin around in my head going what was my fault, what was miscommunication. I think of the times I did something unpleasant to her. I think about how I could have gotten out of it earlier. How I could have made her change. How I could have given her a second chance. How I could have been happy with her. These words, these scenarios play out in my head nearly every day. I am never given peace. I am never left alone. I am not a plastic toy. That's the last thing I am. I am nothing but organic, reactive, of flesh and blood.

Plastic not in the sense that I am of plastic. But that everything around me is plastic. Not only is what surrounds me smooth and ungrippable, it's toxic, it stays around. It swims around in my blood and cerebral fluid. We all breathe out forever chemicals polluting every person we touch and talk to with incessant impurity.

My parents used to go to this Christian camp thing in eastern Norway. We'd rent a caravan and drive across the country. When we were there I didn't really know anyone. I just awkwardly hung out with the children of my parents' friends. One day my mum tells me to go to the caravan of someone. I go, and a boy, no more than a couple years than me says that you have to be 9 to enter. I'm 8. I say that, he says, too bad, you can't enter. I go back to the caravan that my parents rented and play on my nintendo DS.

The next summer, I find the same caravan in the same spot. And I excitedly walk over to the boy, who is standing outside the caravan, almost like a mirror of last time. And I say out loud, so everyone can hear us. I'm 9! I'm finally 9!

Colder than last time, he looks at me and says. "Now you have to be 10".

What does it mean to be good enough? And what do you do when what you need to achieve isn't achievable?

Everything around me feels is plastic. And I am not. Everything is pristine, glossy and neverchanging. I am rotting and stagnating. Everyone around me is 10 years old. I am 9. And next year they're all 11. And I'll merely be 10.

I don't want to die. I want to be made of plastic as well.

Made with spite, hatred and excessive hubris.
