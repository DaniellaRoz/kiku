Aspiring Solipsist

Sometimes intentional self-delusion is the grease which helps the engines of the mind turn.

There is, at least in me, an almost reflex-like behaviour in preventing exposure of something unpleasant to anyone other than myself. Could be as trivial as telling people to stay away from a bad tasting food, or having a high moral need to warn people of genuine dangers. There is, perhaps in a way to lessen my tendency to blame everything on myself, also a desire towards wanting to deny negativity, to deny that bad things are happening. Or at the very least, to pretend they aren't happening. It's much easier to convince yourself that there is no war, than to grapple with the reality that people are senselessly and pointlessly murdered every day. Especially when it weighs on your conscience.

I am not a solipsist, I have never been a solipsist. I distinctly remember a moment where I concieved of it. I didn't know the word solipsism, so I assume I thought of it myself, not that it is a particularly revolutionary worldview. I have a specific vivid memory of telling a classmate, whom I remember specifically who was, about this idea. "What if you were the only conscious mind, and that everyone else was just a part of your imagination". I also remember wrongly thinking stating this to another person was paradoxical in nature.

I am not a solipsist, but I wish I was, because if I was that means that the only pain that is real was mine, and if I well and truly am a solipsist, that would also mean I would no longer feel the weight of the guilt that I push on myself for having hurt others, genuine or imagined. I would feel free from shame and social anxiety, I would truly emancipate myself from society itself.

Solipsism gets called childish, and I think this is why. I think many people are drawn to solipsism, primarily from the emancipation angle. The idea that all of society, all of its norms, rules and dress up is simply a game that you have invented yourself, and through enough mental grit you can manage to push through it. Coupled with a radical rejection of social norms themselves, I think a lot of people are genuinely a little bit solipsist in a way.

The vast majority of people most certainly are not solipsists, but rather in the sense that people put a subconscious value of the self and the in-group above the out group. I think this is a very natural consequence of the cold reality of our world. The strong feed on the weak, and selfishness is strength. In a way, solipsism can become the ultimate form of selfishness. Not just putting yourself above others, but denying the very existence of others. You do not have to help anyone, for you, yes you, are the only being to even exist.

But that's not the angle I take. I want to be a solipsist, not due to a twisted sense of selfishness or a desire to be a social deviant. But because I want to be free from the constant shame that I feel for existing. I want the debt I feel for every time I inhale to vanish. I want the suffering in the world to end. If I was truly the only soul in the world. That would mean there are no people out there suffering in ways I do not suffer. I would feel relieved from my constant dread at the world being an endless machine of suffering.

But then I realise.

I am just as selfish as any other aspiring solipsist, the only difference is that I try to justify it.

I want them to stop suffering so I feel better.

Made with spite, hatred and excessive hubris.

悪意、憎悪、そして過剰な自惚れで作られた。
